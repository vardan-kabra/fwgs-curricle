Safety & Wellbeing Keeping your child physically safe and emotionally supported. +

Well-being at FWGS spans three things — physical safety, emotional health, and a positive place to learn.

CCTV

Cameras across campus exist for one reason: to help keep every student physically safe.

What CCTV helps us do
  • Keep watch over campus grounds and discourage unsafe behaviour
  • Review footage if there's a physical incident, injury, or accident
What CCTV can't do
  • Trace lost or misplaced belongings (see Belongings, Lost & Found)
  • Provide a live watch on everything, everywhere, all the time

FWGS has a large campus, so it isn't possible to cover every area fully — the most important areas are covered.

Physical well-being

  • CCTV and trained staff across campus
  • A campus ambulance on hand for medical emergencies
  • Every physical incident is documented, and we contact you promptly

Emotional & social well-being

  • A dedicated Well-Being Counsellor for all students
  • Teachers trained to notice early signs that a child may be struggling
  • A well-being culture rooted in the IB learner profile — caring, reflective, open-minded

Belongings, Lost & Found What to send, what to label, and what happens if something goes missing. +

We keep a dedicated Lost & Found and will always help you look. A few simple habits at home make lost items far less likely — and easier to recover.

What we'll investigate
  • Academic devices: laptops, tablets, and school-issued equipment
  • May include asking around and reviewing CCTV, decided case by case
What we can't investigate
  • Personal valuables: smartwatches, cash, earphones, footwear, wallets, jewellery
  • Many of these aren't permitted at school anyway — if brought in and lost, we're not able to investigate
  • The school can't take responsibility for personal belongings on campus

Please note: smartwatches, mobiles, or any restricted items brought to school without authorisation will be confiscated.

If something goes missing

  1. Tell the Front Desk promptly
  2. The team checks Lost & Found and coordinates with class teachers
  3. For academic devices, email info@fwgs.in to log an official report

A few habits that help

  • Avoid sending expensive or non-essential items to school
  • Label everything clearly — bags, bottles, stationery, clothing
  • Remind your child that their belongings are their responsibility while on campus

Pillar 1

Your school email ID

Every family gets a school email address in this format:

p.firstname.lastname@fwgs.in

p. = parent · firstname.lastname = your child's name · fwgs.in = the school domain. Students in Grade 6 and above also receive a school email, with an s. prefix in place of p.

Enter your own age, not your child's.

When Google asks for your age at first login, enter your age — not your child's. Google automatically deactivates accounts it believes belong to someone under 13, and reactivating a deactivated account takes 45 days. This is the single most important step when you first sign in.

Official WhatsApp broadcast

The school runs whole-school and grade-wise broadcast lists for short nudges that link back here or to Nucleus. Save our number to receive them. Parent-created WhatsApp groups are not official channels.

Managing your inbox

Primary tab: Must-Know notices and 1:1 emails from teachers or leaders. Promotions: Good-to-Know updates. Classroom category: academic notifications. Treat any personal 1:1 email as a priority — we aim to reply within a working day, and ask the same of you.
